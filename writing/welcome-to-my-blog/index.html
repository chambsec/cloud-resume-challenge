# Welcome to chamb.dev

I spent ten years in clinical healthcare before I wrote a single line of infrastructure code. Radiology, emergency departments, the full chaos of hospital operations during COVID. That part of my life shaped how I think about systems, risk, and what it actually means when something breaks.

I made the move to cloud security engineering a few years ago. Not a career pivot in the abstract sense. More like a lateral step into the infrastructure layer of the same environment I came from. Healthcare IT runs on AWS now. Compliance pipelines, serverless architectures, AI-assisted workflows. I just switched which side of the glass I was working on.

This site is where I write about what I build.

Right now that means production AWS infrastructure: serverless pipelines, compliance automation, security tooling, AI systems. I am not writing tutorials. I am not explaining concepts I have not used in production. Everything here comes from something I actually built, debugged, or burned down and rebuilt.

The topics I come back to are cloud architecture, AWS, security engineering, GRC, healthcare IT, and the process of building products as a solo operator. That last one matters more to me than it probably sounds. There is a specific kind of clarity you develop when you are the only one who can fix it at 2am.

I am not looking for a job. I am building in public.

The first post is already live. "From Clinical to Cloud" covers the transition in more detail if you want the full arc. Everything else I publish here will follow the same pattern: specific, technical, grounded in what actually happened.

If that sounds like something you want to read, stick around.
